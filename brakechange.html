Steps for Changing Your Brake Pads and Rotors

  • List of Tools
  • 1. Jack and jackstands
  • 2. Lug wrench
  • 3. C-clamp
  • 4. Socket set & ratchet
  • 5. Torque wrench
  • 6. Screwdriver
  • 7. Brake cleaner
  • 8. Bungee cord
  • 9. Brake pad spreader
  • 10. Brake grease
  • 11. Blue threadlocker
  • 12. 5lb metal sledge hammer
  • Step 1:Preparation and Safety
    Ensure the vehicle is on a level surface, and lug nuts are loosened. 5 mins
  • Step 2: Jack Up the Car and Remove Wheels
    Fully remove lug nuts using the lug wrench and take off the wheels. 15 mins
  • Step3: Acess Brake Components
    Locate brake caliper and rotor. If needed, remove caliper guide pins or bolts. 10 mins
  • Step 4: Remove Caliper and Brake Pads
    Compress caliper piston, remove caliper, and take out old brake pads. 20 mins
  • Step 5: Remove Old Rotors
    If secured by screws, use a screwdriver or Allen wrench. Take off old rotor. 10 mins
  • Step 6: Clean Components
    Use brake cleaner to clean caliper, bracket, and rotor mounting surfaces. 10 mins
  • Step 7: Install New Rotors and Brake Pads
    Place new rotor onto the hub and secure it with provided screws. Reinstall caliper and new brake bads making sure to apply grease to the contact points. 30 mins
  • Step 8: Test and Check
    Check all connections and fittings. Then place the wheels back on torquing them to spec and go for a slow test drive to ensure the job was done right. 5 mins
